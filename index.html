Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I need to enter a location?

Solar production depends heavily on where you live — how many peak sun hours your area gets per day. PV Calc uses your location to fetch real satellite solar data from the EU JRC PVGIS database, giving you an accurate estimate instead of a generic average.

How accurate are the estimates?

The solar production estimates are based on real satellite irradiance data (PVGIS v5.2) which is the same data used by professional solar installers and researchers worldwide. The financial estimates use your inputs directly. For a final quote, always get bids from at least two or three local installers.

The app shows "offline estimate" — what does that mean?

If the PVGIS API is temporarily unavailable or you have no internet connection, the app falls back to calibrated offline estimates based on historical Hawaii/NSRDB solar data. These are still reasonable estimates but are less precise than live data. Try again with a connection for the most accurate results.

Are the tax credit percentages up to date?

The federal Investment Tax Credit (30%) and Hawaii state tax credit (35%, capped at $5,000) are current as of January 2025. Tax laws can change — always verify current rates using the links in the Costs tab, and consult a tax professional before filing. The app shows the date rates were last verified.

Does the app work outside of Hawaii?

Yes. You can enter any location worldwide that PVGIS covers (most of the Americas, Europe, Africa, and Asia). The solar production estimates will be accurate for your location. Note that the Hawaii state tax credit only applies to Hawaii residents — users in other states should check their local incentives at dsireusa.org.

Does the app use my GPS or store my location?

No. The app does not request location permissions and does not use GPS. You type a city or location manually. That text is sent to OpenStreetMap to find coordinates, which are then sent to PVGIS to retrieve solar data. Nothing is stored on any server. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Can I use this to make a final purchase decision?

PV Calc gives you a solid, data-driven starting point — the same solar resource data professionals use. Use it to compare system sizes, understand payback timelines, and decide whether solar makes sense for your home. Before signing a contract, get multiple quotes from licensed local installers and consult a tax professional about your specific credit eligibility.
